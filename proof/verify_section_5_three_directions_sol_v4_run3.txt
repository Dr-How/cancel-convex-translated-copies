**Bug report**

**Overall verdict:** The solution is **not correct**. It contains multiple **Critical Errors** and **Justification Gaps**.

---

1. **Location:**  
   “*If some vertex \(z\) had smaller distance, a line parallel to \(L\) strictly between \(z\) and both \(x^{-},y^{+}\) would meet the boundary in at least four points, impossible for a convex polygon unless the line contained an edge.*”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   This does not rigorously prove Lemma 2.1. The argument does not justify why such a line must intersect the boundary in four points, nor why the stated exception (“unless the line contained an edge”) cannot occur. So the claimed minimum-area property is not fully established.

2. **Location:**  
   “*\(\operatorname{area}(OAX)=\frac y2\)*” and “*\(\operatorname{area}(BOX)=\frac x2\)*” in Lemma 2.2.

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   These are signed-area formulas. The proof does not explicitly establish that every vertex lies in the relevant supporting half-planes (\(y\ge 0\) for edge \([O,A]\), \(x\ge 0\) for edge \([B,O]\)). Without that, only \(|y|/2\) and \(|x|/2\) are immediate.

3. **Location:**  
   “*But any other vertex of \(S\) has \(y\ge 1\), hence within \([0,1]^2\) it must lie on the top edge \(y=1\). For a point \(X=(x,1)\), … If \(x<1\), this lies on the forbidden ray … Therefore the only possible vertices in a representation of \(q\) are \(A\) and \(O\).*”

   **Description:** **Critical Error.**  
   The case \(X=(1,1)\) is omitted. Under the hypotheses of Lemma 5.1, only the one-sided inequality \(E_i\le E_{i+1}\) is assumed, so Lemma 2.3 cannot be used to exclude the vertex \(R=(1,1)\). If \(R\in S\), then
   \[
   q = R + t_i
   \]
   is an additional representation. The claimed uniqueness of \(q\), and hence the conclusion \(\sigma(q)=\varepsilon(u)\neq 0\), do not follow. This breaks Lemma 5.1.

4. **Location:**  
   “*\(w\notin T\): indeed … \(w-t_i=a_i-a_{i-1}\) lies in the interior of \(C_i\), and it does **not** lie in \(C_{i+1}\), because these two cones occupy disjoint angular sectors for a convex polygon with at least three edge-direction classes.*”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The proof never establishes the claimed angular-separation fact for \(C_i\) and \(C_{i+1}\), nor directly proves that \(a_i-a_{i-1}\notin C_{i+1}\). So the exclusion of the \(s_i\)-representation is not justified here.

5. **Location:**  
   “*Stripping such ends and repeating the same argument gives an inward induction along the block.*”

   **Description:** **Critical Error.**  
   This induction cannot be carried out. For a constant block \(t_b=\cdots=t_c\), one has \(m_b=\cdots=m_{c-1}=0\). After “stripping” the endpoints, the new endpoints are still inside the same constant block, so the hypotheses needed for Lemma 5.1 fail there. For example, at the new left endpoint \(b+1\), one needs \(m_b>0\) to apply Lemma 5.1, but in fact \(m_b=0\).

6. **Location:**  
   “*At that central index the full empty-parallelogram lemma applies, and the same point-construction as in Lemma 5.1 forces the diagonal translate \(t_i+a_i-a_{i-1}\) to lie simultaneously in two incompatible adjacent tangent cones; this is impossible.*”

   **Description:** **Critical Error.**  
   This is asserted without proof, and the cited construction is not available at an interior point of a constant block. There one has \(m_{i-1}=m_i=0\), so the translates
   \[
   u=t_i-a_{i-1},\qquad v=t_i+a_i
   \]
   used in Lemma 5.1 need not belong to \(T\). The claimed contradiction at the “central local minimum” is therefore unsupported.

7. **Location:**  
   “*By Lemma 5.2,
   \[
   m_{i-1}\ge 1,\qquad m_i\ge 1.
   \]
   Hence
   \[
   u:=t_i-a_{i-1}\in F_{i-1},\qquad
   v:=t_i+a_i\in F_i.
   \]*”

   **Description:** **Critical Error.**  
   This is the key input for Sections 6–8, but it relies entirely on Lemma 5.2, which has not been validly proved. So the existence of \(u\) and \(v\), and therefore the final extra-point construction, are unsupported.

Because the proof contains **Critical Errors** (especially in Lemma 5.1 and Lemma 5.2), the solution is **not correct**.
