**The proof structure is not valid as stated.**

Main issues:

1. **It silently adds an extra hypothesis not in the problem.**  
   Subproblem 1 assumes \(g\) is not a single monomial (and hints at conditions like \(|T|\ge 2\) or \(\sum \sigma(t)=0\)). That is **not** part of the stated setup. Under the given setup, a single translate with sign \(+1\) gives exactly \(n\) nonzero points for **any** convex-position \(S\), so the outline is not addressing the original statement.

2. **Signed-multiset degeneracies are not covered.**  
   The plan treats extreme coefficients as if they were automatically \(\pm1\), but \(T\) is a **multiset**: repeated translation vectors with opposite signs can cancel. So the decomposition in Subproblem 1 is not aligned with the actual hypotheses.

3. **Several steps assume \(S\) is 2-dimensional, but that is never established.**  
   Subproblem 3 and the final paragraph assume \(\operatorname{Conv}(S)\) is a polygon (\(n\ge 3\)). The original problem allows \(n=1,2\), and the outline never proves equality forces the 2D case. So there are missing cases.

4. **The outline constrains \(h\) and \(\operatorname{Conv}(S)+\operatorname{Conv}(T)\), not \(S\) itself.**  
   Subproblems 4 and 5 only yield information about the support of \(h\), i.e. the vertices of \(\operatorname{Conv}(S+T)\). Even if that polygon has 4 vertices or is a parallelogram, there is no lemma transferring that structure back to \(S\).

5. **The final inference “therefore \(S\) is a parallelogram” is unsupported.**  
   Earlier lemmas only say edges of \(T\) are parallel to edges of \(S\), or that \(h\) factors by \((1-X^v)\). That does **not** establish that \(S\) has exactly two edge directions or opposite sides parallel. A crucial concluding lemma is missing.

6. **At best the plan gets \(n\le 4\), not the stated classification.**  
   Subproblem 5 bounds the number of paired segments, hence \(n\le 4\). But the target is stronger: equality should force **\(S\) itself** to be a parallelogram. That stronger conclusion is not obtained by the listed subproblems.

7. **Subproblem 3 depends on a major external classification theorem whose role is not properly integrated.**  
   The outline invokes a strong Newton-polytope/Ostrowski-type result with hypotheses not clearly tracked from earlier subproblems. So the dependency chain is not clean, especially if the goal is to prove the conjecture “from scratch.”

There is no obvious major redundancy; most subproblems would be relevant in a repaired proof. The problem is not extra lemmas, but **missing cases and a missing bridge from properties of \(h\) / \(S+T\) to a structural conclusion about \(S\)**.
