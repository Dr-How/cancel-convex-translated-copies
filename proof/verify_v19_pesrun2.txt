ISSUE 1 [Critical Error] — Direction-class definition mismatch
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3
  Location: "Direction classes. [a]=[b] iff b=λa for some λ≠0."
  The proof silently replaces the problem's equivalence relation (positive scalar
  multiples) with the coarser equivalence (arbitrary nonzero scalar multiples), thereby
  conflating opposite-direction edge pairs. The parallelogram remark "2 classes" is
  only correct under the coarser definition. The proof is therefore addressing a
  different claim from the one in the problem statement.

ISSUE 2 [Critical Error] — Distinctness of support points p_i not proved
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3, run1_tmp
  Location: "Since the p_i are distinct (the s_i are distinct and the t_i come from
  disjoint normal cones) and |supp(σ̃)|=n: supp(σ̃)={p_i=t_i+s_i}"
  The parenthetical reasoning does not establish that t_i+s_i are distinct: distinct
  s_i and disjoint normal cones do not prevent t_i+s_i=t_j+s_j for i≠j. The
  identification of the full support with {p_i} is used throughout the proof (e.g.
  every application of (VAN)), so this gap propagates everywhere.

ISSUE 3 [Critical Error] — m=0 case not correctly excluded
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3, run1_tmp
  Location: "If m=0: t_1=t_2=t_3, so T⊆{t_1}, contradicting both signs present."
  From m_1=m_2=m_3=0 one deduces t_1=t_2=t_3 and F_i={t_1}. This says nothing about
  additional translate points of T outside those three edge chains. T⊆{t_1} is not
  established and the exclusion of m=0 is therefore invalid.

ISSUE 4 [Justification Gap] — "Cyclic relabeling" argument for consecutive edges
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3, run1_tmp
  Location: "by cyclic relabeling we may take them to include edges 1,2,3"
  Cyclic relabeling only rotates the edge index sequence. It does not guarantee that
  three arbitrary edges belonging to three distinct direction classes can always be
  made consecutive. The entire height construction that follows uses a_1,a_2,a_3 as
  specific consecutive edges (with s_3=a_1+a_2), so this missing justification is load-
  bearing.

ISSUE 5 [Critical Error] — Lemma betamin is not proved (misapplication of (F1))
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3, run1_tmp
  Location: "By (F1), t_1 is the unique maximiser of ⟨·,−v⟩=−β(·) over T,
  i.e. t_1 minimises β over T."
  (F1) governs the translate whose normal cone contains the direction -v. But -v is
  only the outward normal to edge [s_1,s_2], not a direction that uniquely exposes the
  vertex s_1. When m_1>0, t_2=t_1+m_1a_1 also satisfies β(t_2)=β(t_1), so t_1 is
  not a unique minimizer. Lemma betamin is false as stated, and every subsequent use
  of h(t)≥0 is therefore unsupported.

ISSUE 6 [Critical Error] — Closure argument erroneously collapses parallel edges
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3, run1_tmp
  Location: "The closure reduces to m_1a_1+∑_{[a_i]=[a_1], i≠1} m_i a_i=0,
  i.e. (∑_{[a_i]=[a_1]} m_i)a_1=0, forcing all m_i=0."
  Edges in the same direction class need only satisfy a_i=λ_i a_1 for some scalar λ_i;
  they are not required to equal a_1 exactly. The closure sum is (m_1+∑λ_i m_i)a_1,
  not (∑m_i)a_1. The collapse is invalid, so the proof of T⊄F_1 and hence the
  existence of a translate with h>0 are not established.

ISSUE 7 [Critical Error] — Coordinate decomposition for c_j=h_0 is wrong
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3, run1_tmp
  Location: "For each j with c_j=h_0, write t'−s_j=λ_j a_1+h_0 v …
  Then t'+ra_1−s_j=(r+λ_j)a_1+h_0 v, which lies in F_1 iff …" and eq. (Dform).
  When c_j=h_0, h(t'−s_j)=h_0−c_j=0, so t'−s_j lies on the height-zero line
  t_1+ℝa_1, not on a line with v-component h_0. The formula ignores the necessary
  affine shift by t_1, and the coefficient of v was never identified with the height.
  The membership test for F_1, eq. (Dform), and the subsequent bound R* are all
  unsupported.

ISSUE 8 [Critical Error] — Cancellation fallacy: one nonzero D-term does not imply
  nonzero D-sum (Sub-case B1)
  Flagged by: pes_run1, pes_run2, pes_run3, run1_tmp
  Location: "The j_0-term in D(t'+(R_max+2)a_1) is … ≠0. Hence
  σ_T(t'+(R_max+2)a_1)=−D(…) contains the nonzero summand … In particular,
  |σ_T(t'+(R_max+2)a_1)|≥1."
  A sum of terms that includes one nonzero summand can still total zero due to
  cancellation by other terms. No argument eliminates the possibility of cancellation.
  The conclusion that t'+(R_max+2)a_1∈T does not follow.

ISSUE 9 [Critical Error] — Same cancellation fallacy in Sub-case B2

ISSUE 11 [Critical Error] — p_{j_0}=q* identification is invalid
