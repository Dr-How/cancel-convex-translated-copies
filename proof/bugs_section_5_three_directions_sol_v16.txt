[Run1, Run2, Run3] CRITICAL — Wrong theorem is proved: The proof assumes T itself
  is an n-vertex convex polygon / support of a signed measure sigma_T, and that the
  relevant edge directions belong to T. The stated problem has edge directions belonging
  to P=conv(S), with T an arbitrary finite translate set. No argument is given that the
  edge-direction classes of any structure built from T coincide with those of P. The
  argument therefore proves a different statement from the one required.

[Run2, Run3] CRITICAL — Direction-class notion changed: The proof uses equivalence
  under nonzero scalar multiples for direction classes, but the problem uses equivalence
  under positive scalar multiples only. These differ: a_3 could be a negative multiple
  of a_1, giving [a_3]!=[a_1] under the problem's definition yet beta(a_3)=0 under the
  proof's linear functional. The key inference "Since [a_3]!=[a_1], mu:=beta(a_3)!=0"
  is therefore invalid.

[Run1, Run2, Run3] CRITICAL — Sub-case B2 argument is logically false: The proof
  claims "All delta_j=0 for j in J_0 => D_2=D_1=0: identical to Case A." But D_2 is
  defined as sum of delta_j, while D_1 is defined as sum of delta'_j where
  delta'_j = sigma_T(t'-s_j) != delta_j = sigma_T(t'+s_2-s_j). Vanishing of all
  delta_j does not imply vanishing of all delta'_j. Sub-case B2 is therefore not proved,
  and the claim "identical to Case A" does not hold when J_0 is nonempty.

[Run1, Run2, Run3] CRITICAL — False inference about chain lengths: The proof asserts
  "by sum m_i=n>=4 and m_i>=0: some m_j>=1 for j!=1." This is false: all of the total
  sum could be concentrated in m_1, i.e., m_1=n, m_j=0 for all j!=1. No argument
  excludes this case.

[Run1, Run2, Run3] JUSTIFICATION GAP — Foundational identities (NL) and (S0) not
  derived: The proof uses throughout a new object sigma_T and identities labelled (NL)
  and (S0). These are neither among the facts allowed from Subproblems 1-4 nor proved in
  this solution. The entire chain argument rests on unestablished foundations.

[Run1, Run2, Run3] JUSTIFICATION GAP — Existence of height functional beta and
  positivity of c_j not proved: The proof asserts the existence of a linear functional
  beta with beta(a_1)=0, beta(a_2)=1, h(p)=beta(p-t_1)>=0 for all p in T (citing t_1
  as a vertex of conv(T)), and then claims c_j=h(x_j)>0 for j>=3. Neither the vertex
  property of t_1 nor the strict positivity of c_j is established from the given
  hypotheses. These claims underlie every subsequent height argument.

[Run1, Run2, Run3] JUSTIFICATION GAP — T=union of chains F_i not proved: The proof
  repeatedly concludes membership and non-membership in T by checking only the chains
  F_i, treating T=F_1 cup ... cup F_n as a given. This covering property is never
  established and is not among the allowed facts. As a consequence, the contradiction
  for n=3 (deducing t_1+a_2 not in T) does not follow.

[Run1, Run2, Run3] JUSTIFICATION GAP — Iteration of (S0) along a_1-line not

[Run1] JUSTIFICATION GAP — Identity sum m_i=n used but not proved: The relation
  sum_{i=1}^n m_i = n is used crucially (e.g., for n=3 to conclude m=1), but it is not

[Run3] CRITICAL — Three-direction hypothesis unused in n>=4 case: In Section 2

SUMMARY OF MOST CRITICAL ISSUE
===========================================================================

The most critical issue: Sub-case B2 ("All delta_j=0") is handled by asserting
"D_2=D_1=0: identical to Case A," but this is a direct logical error — D_1 and D_2
involve different quantities (delta'_j vs. delta_j), so D_2=0 does not entail D_1=0.
This gap means one entire branch of the case analysis is unproved regardless of whether
the surrounding framework were otherwise sound.
