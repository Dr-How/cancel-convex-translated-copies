1. **Location:** “\(e_i:=v_{i+1}-v_i\qquad (1\le i\le n,\; v_{n+1}=v_1)\)” and later “For each \(i\), let \(\varepsilon_i\) be the counterclockwise turning angle from \(e_i\) to \(e_{i+1}\) …”
   
   **Description — Justification Gap:** The proof uses \(e_{i+1}\) cyclically, but only \(e_1,\dots,e_n\) are defined; \(e_{n+1}\) is never defined. As written, \(\varepsilon_n\) is not formally defined, and the later sum \(\sum_{i=1}^n \varepsilon_i\) is therefore not fully well-specified.

2. **Location:** “Since we are traversing the polygon counterclockwise, \(\varepsilon_i>0\). Also, \(\varepsilon_i\neq\pi\) … Hence
\[
0<\varepsilon_i<\pi.
\]”

   **Description — Justification Gap:** The displayed inference is not justified. From \(\varepsilon_i>0\) and \(\varepsilon_i\neq \pi\), one cannot conclude \(\varepsilon_i<\pi\); angles \(>\pi\) have not been excluded. So the proof does not actually establish that these \(\varepsilon_i\) are the exterior angles to which the next angle-sum theorem is being applied.

3. **Location:** “Choose an argument \(\theta_1\) of \(e_1\), and define recursively
\[
\theta_{i+1}:=\theta_i+\varepsilon_i.
\]
Then
\[
\theta_1<\theta_2<\cdots<\theta_n
\]
… So the directed edge directions occur in strictly increasing order during one full turn.”

   **Description — Justification Gap:** The proof never explicitly shows that each \(\theta_i\) is an argument of \(e_i\). Without that identification, the monotonicity of the numbers \(\theta_i\) does not yet justify the conclusion that the actual edge directions of \(e_1,\dots,e_n\) are strictly increasing/distinct.

4. **Location:** “Thus there exist numbers \(a,b,c,d>0\) and signs \(\sigma,\tau\in\{\pm1\}\) such that
\[
B-A=a\,u,\qquad C-B=b\,w,\qquad D-C=\sigma c\,u,\qquad A-D=\tau d\,w.

   **Description — Justification Gap:** Earlier, \(u\) and \(w\) were fixed only as arbitrary representatives of the two direction classes. This line assumes, without justification, that \(u\) has been oriented to point along \(AB\) and \(w\) along \(BC\), so that the first two coefficients can be taken positive with no extra signs. That reorientation is not stated.

Because of these gaps, the proof does **not** meet the stated standard of a complete verification.
