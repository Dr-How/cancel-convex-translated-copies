The solution is **not correct**. It contains multiple **Critical Errors** and **Justification Gaps**.

## Bug report

1. **Location:**  
   “Since \(m_k=0\), the face chain \(F_k=\{t_k\}=\{t_0\}\), so \(t_0\) maximises \(\langle\cdot,u\rangle\) over \(T\) (the maximiser is achieved by \(F_k\)).”  
   **Description — Justification Gap:**  
   The allowed facts only give uniqueness of \(t_i\) for a specific \(u_i\) in the **interior** of the normal cone, plus the arithmetic-progression description of \(F_i\). No result is stated showing that for an **arbitrary** \(u\in \mathcal N(s_k)\), the maximizers of \(\langle\cdot,u\rangle\) over \(T\) lie on \(F_k\). This step is used to prove \(T=\{t_0\}\), but it is not justified.

2. **Location:**  
   In Lemma \(\ref{lem:step}\):  
   \[
   \sigma_T(t+a_1+a_2)+\sigma_T(t+a_2)+\sigma(t)+\sigma_T(t+a_3)=0.
   \]  
   **Description — Critical Error:**  
   This is the \(n=3\) case. Applying \(\eqref{eq:nonlonely}\) to \(p=s_3+t\) should produce only **three** terms (for \(j=1,2,3\)). The displayed equation has a spurious fourth term \(\sigma_T(t+a_3)\). The staircase lemma and the subsequent contradiction for \(n=3\) are built on this incorrect equation.

3. **Location:**  
   In Lemma \(\ref{lem:betamin4}\):  
   “For \(j\ge 3\): heights \(h^*-c_j\le h^*-1<h^*=h_{\min}\), so terms vanish.”  
   **Description — Justification Gap:**  
   Earlier, the proof explicitly established only \(c_j>0\) in general, not \(c_j\ge 1\). The displayed inequality uses an unproved stronger bound. As written, the vanishing of the \(j\ge 3\) terms is not justified.

4. **Location:**  
   “The face-chain height set is
   \[
   \mathcal H:=\{0,1,\dots,m_2\}\cup\{m_2+k\mu: k=1,\dots,m_3\}
   \cup\{(m_4-k)(1+\mu): k=0,\dots,m_4\},
   \]
   ... By the \(\beta\)-closure: \(m_2+m_3\mu=m_4(1+\mu)\) ... and
   \(\beta(a_4)=\beta(-(a_1+a_2+a_3))\).”  
   **Description — Critical Error:**  
   Section 2 is stated for all \(n\ge 4\), but this formula assumes \(a_4=-(a_1+a_2+a_3)\), which is true only when \(n=4\). For \(n\ge 5\), the definition of \(\mathcal H\) omits the later chains \(F_5,\dots,F_n\), and the cited closure identity is false.

5. **Location:**  
   In Lemma \(\ref{lem:nogaps}\):  
   “By induction on \(h\) ... Base case \(h\in(0,\min(\mu_+,1))\) ... Inductive step: Assume no \(T\)-elements at heights in \((0,h_0)\setminus\mathcal H\) ...”  
   **Description — Justification Gap:**  
   This is not a valid induction scheme as written. Heights are real numbers, and no well-ordered discrete parameter is specified. The proof also mixes this with unproved assertions such as “\(h-c_j\notin\mathcal H\) by the structure of \(\mathcal H\).”

6. **Location:**  
   In Remark (1):  
   “For \(\mu\in\mathbb{Q}_{>0}\setminus\mathbb{Z}\): one checks that the resulting \(k\) exceeds \(m_4\) or is negative ... and we omit the arithmetic details. Therefore:
   \[
   \eqref{eq:a3vanish}.
   \]”  
   **Description — Justification Gap:**  
   The proof explicitly omits the arithmetic needed to establish \(\eqref{eq:a3vanish}\), yet later arguments rely on \(\eqref{eq:a3vanish}\) as if it were proved.

7. **Location:**  
   In Lemma \(\ref{lem:top}\):  
   “For \(\mu\notin\mathbb{Z}_{>0}\): \(m_2+1\) is not a face-chain height ... so both \(\sigma_T(t+a_2)=\sigma_T(t+a_1+a_2)=0\).”  
   **Description — Critical Error:**  
   This claim is false in general. For example, if \(\mu=\tfrac12\) and \(m_3\ge 2\), then \(m_2+1=m_2+2\mu\), which is a height on \(F_3\), so \(m_2+1\in\mathcal H\). The vanishing of the height-\(m_2+1\) terms is therefore invalid.

8. **Location:**  
   Also in Lemma \(\ref{lem:top}\):

   **Description — Justification Gap:**  
   No complete argument is given for \(m_2-\mu\notin\mathcal H\). The proof merely refers back to earlier “closure analysis,” which itself was not fully carried out.

9. **Location:**  
   In Proposition \(\ref{prop:n4mu}\), case “\(m_1=0,\ m_2\ge 2\)”:  
   “Heights: \(2,\ 2,\ 1,\ 1-\mu<0\) (since \(\mu>0\), last term \(=0\)).”  
   **Description — Critical Error:**  
   From \(\mu>0\) it does **not** follow that \(1-\mu<0\). This is false when \(0<\mu<1\). The contradiction in this case uses an incorrect vanishing claim.

10. **Location:**

   **Description — Critical Error:**

11. **Location:**

12. **Location:**
