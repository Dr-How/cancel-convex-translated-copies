**Verdict: the proof structure is not fully valid as written.**

The high-level strategy is sensible —  
global translate-cycle structure \(\to\) extract a local three-direction block \(\to\) derive a local obstruction \(\to\) classify the remaining polygons —  
but there are logical gaps in how the contradiction is obtained.

### Issues

1. **Missing identification of the \(n\) “already counted” nonzero points.**  
   Subproblem 3 ends with an alternative of the form
   \[
   \exists p\notin \{q_1,\dots,q_n\}\text{ with }\sigma(p)\neq 0,
   \quad q_r=s_r+t_r.
   \]
   For this to contradict \(|\{p:\sigma(p)\neq 0\}|=n\), the outline needs a prior fact that:
   - the \(q_r\) are \(n\) distinct points, and
   - each \(q_r\) already satisfies \(\sigma(q_r)\neq 0\).
   
   That fact is not among the listed prior results and is not proved in the outline.

2. **The overlap outcomes in Subproblem 3 are not yet shown to be contradictory.**  
   Subproblem 4 says that any nontrivial chain overlap “violates the boundary geometry,” but no earlier subproblem actually establishes the needed geometric property of the translate cycle \(Q=(t_i)\) — e.g. that it is simple/convex, or that a translate cannot lie on two different chains except at the expected shared endpoint.

   In particular, the branch
   \[
   F_i\cap F_{i+2}\neq\varnothing
   \]
   is **not** automatically a contradiction from the stated earlier lemmas alone.

3. **Because of (2), Subproblem 3 is too weak for Subproblem 4 as stated.**  
   The conclusion of Subproblem 3 is a disjunction:
   - some branches are immediate geometric impossibilities only if extra facts about \(Q\) are available,
   - one branch gives an extra nonzero point.

   But Subproblem 4 needs **every** branch to force a contradiction. As written, that bridge is missing.  
   So either:
   - Subproblem 3 must be strengthened to conclude directly “extra nonzero point” or “impossible repeated translate,” or
   - an additional lemma is needed converting each overlap case into such a contradiction.

4. **Minor relevance/dependency issue: the parity conclusion from Subproblem 1 is not explicitly used later.**  
   Subproblem 1 proves the evenness of \(\sum_i m_i\), but the later subproblems do not clearly invoke it. This is not fatal, but as written it looks partly unused.

---

### Bottom line

The decomposition has the **right overall shape**, but it is **not logically complete** with the currently stated subproblems. The main missing piece is an intermediate lemma connecting the overlap alternatives in Subproblem 3 to an actual contradiction with the extremal equality.
