1. **Justification Gap**  
   **Location:** “Since the \(p_i\) are distinct (the \(s_i\) are distinct and the \(t_i\) come from disjoint normal cones) and \(|\mathrm{supp}(\tilde\sigma)|=n\):  
   \[

   \]”  
   **Description:** The stated reason does not prove that the \(p_i\) are distinct. From \(s_i\neq s_j\) and different associated translates \(t_i,t_j\), it does **not** follow that \(t_i+s_i\neq t_j+s_j\). The preceding claim that all off-diagonal terms vanish at \(p_i\) is also only sketched. Since later applications of (VAN) rely on the identification \(\mathrm{supp}(\tilde\sigma)=\{p_i\}\), this is a foundational gap.

2. **Critical Error**

   **Description:** From \(m_1=m_2=m_3=0\) one only gets \(t_1=t_2=t_3\). The allowed facts do **not** imply that \(T\) has no other elements besides these exposed translates. Therefore the deduction \(m\ge1\) (and then \(m\ge2\)) is invalid, and Step 3 of the triangle case is unsupported.

   **Description:** Cyclic relabeling alone does not make arbitrary chosen edges consecutive. An additional argument is needed to show that a polygon with at least three direction classes has some consecutive triple of edges with pairwise distinct classes. That argument is not given.

4. **Critical Error**

5. **Critical Error**
