**Verdict:** The proof structure is **essentially valid for the stated conjecture** (the one-way implication: equality \(\Rightarrow n=4\) and \(S\) is a parallelogram), but it has a few **structural clean-up issues**.

### What works structurally
- The main dependency chain is sound:
  - **Subproblem 1** gives the lower bound and the extremal interpretation of equality.
  - **Subproblem 2** provides the geometric overlap cap.
  - **Subproblem 3** reduces the general signed family to exactly two translates.
  - **Subproblem 4** converts that reduction into the bound \(n\le 4\).
  - Then the remaining small-\(n\) classification yields \(n=4\) and the quadrilateral structure.
- There is **no obvious missing case** for the stated conjecture once \(n\ge 3\) is assumed.

### Issues
1. **Subproblem 5 is redundant.**  
   From Subproblem 4, under equality,
   \[
   n = 2n - 2|S\cap(S+v)| \quad\Rightarrow\quad |S\cap(S+v)| = n/2.
   \]
   Together with Subproblem 2, this gives \(n/2\le 2\), hence \(n\le 4\), and also shows \(n\) must be even. Since the conjecture assumes \(n\ge 3\), this already forces \(n=4\). So the separate triangle exclusion is not needed.

2. **Subproblem 6 is redundant as stated, given Subproblem 2.**  
   The second clause of Subproblem 2 already says that if \(|S\cap(S+v)|=2\), the four involved points form a parallelogram. Once \(n=4\), those are all the points of \(S\), so Subproblem 6 is already implied.  
   - If you want Subproblem 6 to be genuinely needed, then Subproblem 2 should be weakened to only the bound \(\le 2\).

3. **The final sentence overstates the conclusion.**  
   The integration sketch ends with:
   > “Thus equality with both signs occurs exactly in the parallelogram case...”
   
   But the listed subproblems only prove the **necessary condition** in the conjecture. They do **not** include a converse/existence step showing that every parallelogram actually attains equality for some signed \(T\).  
   - This does **not** hurt the proof of the stated conjecture.
   - It only means the final sentence is stronger than what the outline has structurally established.

### Bottom line
- **For the stated conjecture:** the structure is **basically sound**.
- **As written:** it is **not perfectly clean**, because of:
  - redundant Subproblems **5** and **6**, and
  - an **overstrong concluding claim** not justified by the listed steps.
