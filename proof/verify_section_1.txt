**Bug report**

1. **Location:** “Since \(T\) is finite, choose \(t_s\in T\) with
   \[
   \phi_s(t_s)=\max_{t\in T}\phi_s(t).
   \]”

   **Description — Critical Error:**  
   This step silently assumes \(T\neq\varnothing\). Finiteness alone does **not** justify choosing an element of \(T\) or taking \(\max_{t\in T}\) if \(T\) may be empty. Under the stated hypotheses, \(T=\varnothing\) is not excluded. In that case this construction is impossible, and in fact the statements being proved are false (\(F\equiv 0\), so the lower bound \(|\{p:F(p)\neq 0\}|\ge n\) fails when \(n>0\)). The same hidden assumption reappears later when \(M_T\) and \(t^\ast\) are chosen in part (6).

2. **Location:**  
   - “The only standard convex-geometric input I use is that every vertex of a polyhedron is an exposed point…”  
   - “For each \(s\in S\), choose a linear functional \(\phi_s\colon\mathbb R^2\to\mathbb R\) that is uniquely maximized at \(s\) on \(P=\operatorname{conv}(S)\)…”
   - “choose a linear functional \(\psi:\mathbb R^2\to\mathbb R\) that is uniquely maximized at \(v\) on \(P+Q\).”

   **Description — Justification Gap:**  
   The cited external result does state that every vertex of a **polyhedron** is an exposed point. ([ms.uky.edu](https://www.ms.uky.edu/~Lee/ma714fa13/notes.pdf)) But the solution never explicitly verifies the hypotheses needed to apply that theorem to \(P=\operatorname{conv}(S)\) and especially to \(P+Q\). If the argument is relying on that external proposition, it must also justify that these sets are in the class covered by the theorem. As written, that applicability check is missing.

Because of the critical error above, the solution is **not fully valid as written** under the stated hypotheses.
