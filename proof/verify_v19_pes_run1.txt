1. **Critical Error**  
   **Location:** “\([a]=[b]\) iff \(b=\lambda a\) for some \(\lambda\ne0\). (A parallelogram has edges \(a_1,a_2,-a_1,-a_2\) giving exactly 2 classes.)”  
   **Description:** This changes the problem’s stated notion of direction class from **positive** scalar multiples to **arbitrary nonzero** scalar multiples. Relative to the statement as written, the proof is proving a different claim.

2. **Justification Gap**  
   **Location:** “Since the \(p_i\) are distinct (the \(s_i\) are distinct and the \(t_i\) come from disjoint normal cones) and \(|\mathrm{supp}(\tilde\sigma)|=n\):  
   \[
   \mathrm{supp}(\tilde\sigma)=\{p_i=t_i+s_i:1\le i\le n\}.
   \]”  
   **Description:** The stated reason does not prove the \(p_i\) are distinct. From \(s_i\neq s_j\) and the way \(t_i\) are chosen, it does not follow that \(t_i+s_i\neq t_j+s_j\). This exact support identification is used later, but it is not established here.

3. **Critical Error**

   **Description:** From \(t_1=t_2=t_3\) one cannot conclude \(T\) has no other elements. The allowed facts describe the chains \(F_i\), not all of \(T\). So the case \(m=0\) is not actually excluded.

4. **Justification Gap**

   **Description:** No proof is given that three distinct direction classes can be arranged at consecutive indices \(1,2,3\) by cyclic relabeling alone. The later height-functional setup depends on this.

5. **Critical Error**

   **Description:** \(-v\) is the outward normal to the **edge** \([s_1,s_2]\), not a functional exposing the vertex \(s_1\). Nothing in (F1) justifies unique maximization for this functional. In fact the proof later claims \(F_1\subseteq\{h=0\}\), so every point of \(F_1\) has the same \(\beta\)-value as \(t_1\). Thus Lemma “betamin” is not established.

6. **Critical Error**
