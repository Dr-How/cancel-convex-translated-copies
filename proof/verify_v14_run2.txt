--- CRITICAL ERRORS ---

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-01: Wrong object studied throughout.
  Location: Opening setup — "We have x_1,...,x_n in convex position (CCW),
  sigma_T finitely supported on T = {x_1,...,x_n}."
  The problem has S as the convex n-gon and T as the signed translate set.
  The proof identifies T with the convex polygon and with the support of sigma.
  Every subsequent deduction is about the wrong object.

[Run2,Run3] ISSUE-02: Direction class definition silently widened.
  Location: "The direction class [a_i] is the equivalence class under nonzero
  scalar multiples."
  The problem uses positive scalar multiples. Enlarging to all nonzero multiples
  is not proved equivalent and changes the count.

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-03: (S0) equation has wrong term count for n=3.
  Location: n=3 lemma, equation applied with k=2:
    sigma_T(t'+a_1)+sigma_T(t')+sigma_T(t'-a_2)+sigma_T(t'-a_2-a_3)=0.
  For n=3, (S0) has only THREE terms. The fourth term is spurious; since
  t'-a_2-a_3=t'+a_1, the coefficient-2 relation that follows is invalid.
  (This error appears twice in the n=3 section.)

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-04: n=3 case split abandoned.
  Location: "This is getting complicated. Let me use the cleaner approach:
  for n=3, ANY t' in T with h(t')>0 works."
  The h(t')=0 sub-case is never resolved and existence of an opposite-sign point
  at positive height is never established.

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-05: Invalid vanishing of last term in Lemma hzero (n=4).
  Location: "For n=4: sigma_T(q+a_1)+sigma_T(q)+0+0=0 (heights 0,0,-1,-1-mu)."
  If mu<0, then -1-mu >= 0, so the fourth term need not vanish. The two-term
  relation is derived from a truncated equation.

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-06: (NL) applied at a point miscomputed in Lemma cross.
  Location: "apply (NL) at p = s_1+(t_2+a_2) = t_1+a_2."
  Since t_2=t_1+m_1*a_1, we have s_1+(t_2+a_2)=t_1+m_1*a_1+a_2, not t_1+a_2
  (unless m_1=0). The cross-point equation is evaluated at the wrong location.

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-07: (NL) applied without establishing p not in support.
  Location: Lemma cross — "possible but a degenerate subcase; in the
  non-degenerate case p not in T."
  The proof explicitly admits p may lie in the support T. Applicability of (NL)
  is never established; degenerate cases are silently dropped.

[Run2] ISSUE-08: Proof identifies t_1+a_2 = t_2+a_2 when m_1>=1.
  Location: Lemma cross — "sigma_T(t_1+a_2) = sigma_T(t_2+a_2)."
  The same paragraph notes t_1+a_2 != t_2+a_2 when m_1>=1. These two points
  cannot be identified.

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-09: Lemmas cross and cross1 stated for all mu but proved
  only for mu>0, then used for all non-integer mu.
  Location: "So Lemma cross1 is proved for mu>0. (The case mu<0 is handled in
  Prop n4muZ.)" — but Prop n4muZ handles integer mu, leaving negative
  non-integer mu uncovered. Proposition n4mu subsequently cites these lemmas
  as if fully proved.

[Run2,Run3] ISSUE-10: False arithmetic claim in Prop n4mu.
  Location: "If mu is rational non-integer: mu=p/q with q>=2; then
  mu=1/(k_0-1-j) requires q=1: contradiction."
  Counterexample: mu=1/2 is rational non-integer and equals 1/2=1/(k_0-1-j)
  for k_0-1-j=2. The exclusion of a face-chain height based on this step is
  invalid.

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-11: c_j > 0 does not imply -c_j <= -1 (n>=5 section).
  Location: Lemma hzero-n — "extra j>=5 terms have heights -c_j <= -1 < 0
  (for mu<0: c_j>0 by convexity)."
  c_j>0 only gives -c_j<0, not -c_j<=-1. The vanishing of extra terms in the
  n>=5 case is therefore not established for mu<0.

[Run1,Run2,Run3] ISSUE-12: Final conclusion addresses the wrong object.
  Location: "Therefore, T can have at most 2 edge-direction classes."
  The theorem to prove is about P=conv(S). This confirms the entire proof
  was about T, not S.
