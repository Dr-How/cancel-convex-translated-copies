## Bug report

1. **Location:**  
   “So \(s_i\) is the unique maximizer of \(\langle u_i-\varepsilon a_i,\cdot\rangle\) on \(P\), i.e.
   \[
   u_i-\varepsilon a_i\in C_i.
   \]
   … Hence
   \[
   u_i+\varepsilon a_i\in C_{i+1}.
   \]”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The argument only compares the linear functional at the vertices \(s_j\). It does not explicitly justify why this implies unique maximization on all of \(P=\operatorname{conv}(S)\). That requires a stated theorem or a convex-combination argument.

2. **Location:**  
   “Thus \(t_i\) maximizes \(\langle u_i,\cdot\rangle\) on \(T\), hence on \(Q=\operatorname{conv}(T)\). Therefore
   \[
   t_i\in G_i.
   \]
   Exactly the same argument … shows that
   \[
   t_{i+1}\in G_i.
   \]”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The passage from “maximizes on \(T\)” to “maximizes on \(Q=\operatorname{conv}(T)\)” is not justified. Preliminary Fact 3 only proves the **unique**-maximizer version, and uniqueness at \(u_i\) was not established here.

3. **Location:**  
   “Therefore the two endpoints of the segment \(G_i\) are exactly \(t_i\) and \(t_{i+1}\). Hence
   \[
   G_i=[t_i,t_{i+1}].
   \]”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The proof shows \(t_i\) is the unique minimizer and \(t_{i+1}\) the unique maximizer of \(\langle a_i,\cdot\rangle\) on \(G_i\), but it does not explicitly justify that for a compact convex subset of a line parallel to \(a_i\), those must be the segment’s endpoints.

4. **Location:**  
   “Also, by Subproblems \(1\)–\(2\), the points \(\ell_i=x_i\) are the \(n\) support points in the extremal case, so they are pairwise distinct.”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The setup explicitly allows using that each \(t_i\) is the unique maximizer on \(C_i\) and that \(\ell_i=s_i+t_i\) is the corresponding support point. It does **not** explicitly state that these support points are pairwise distinct. This imports an additional prior result without stating it.

5. **Location:**  
   “For the polygon \(P\), every nonzero direction lies either in one of the open vertex cones \(C_1,\dots,C_n\), or on one of the \(n\) boundary rays determined by the edge normals \(u_1,\dots,u_n\). The union of those boundary rays has empty interior, so the open cone \(D_y\) meets some \(C_i\).”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   This is a structural statement about the normal fan of a convex polygon, but it is neither proved nor cited. The later conclusion \(D_y\cap C_i\neq\varnothing\) depends on this unstated theorem.

6. **Location:**  
   “Hence
   \[
   [s_i,s_{i+1}]+[t_i,t_{i+1}]
   =
   \{x_i+(\alpha+\lambda_i\beta)a_i:0\le \alpha,\beta\le 1\}
   =
   [x_i,x_{i+1}],
   \]
   because \(x_{i+1}=x_i+(1+\lambda_i)a_i\).”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The equality with the full segment \([x_i,x_{i+1}]\) is not fully justified. One must explicitly show that
   \[
   \{\alpha+\lambda_i\beta:\alpha,\beta\in[0,1]\}=[0,1+\lambda_i]
   \]
   when \(\lambda_i\ge 0\).

7. **Location:**  
   “Since the vertices are exactly the pairwise distinct points \(x_1,\dots,x_n\), this means they occur on the boundary in the cyclic order
   \[
   x_1,x_2,\dots,x_n.
   \]”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   Even after showing each \([x_i,x_{i+1}]\) is a boundary edge, the proof does not explicitly justify why this forces the claimed cyclic order. This sentence also relies on the earlier unsupported claim that the \(x_i\) are pairwise distinct.

Because of these gaps, the solution does **not** meet the “no Critical Errors and zero Justification Gaps” standard.
