\paragraph{Proof.}
By Subproblem $2$,
\[
I=-\int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi/2}\Xi(\theta,\psi)\,\rho(\theta,\psi)\,\sin\theta\,d\psi\,d\theta.
\]
By Subproblem $3$, $\Xi(\theta,\psi)\ge 0$ almost everywhere on $(0,\pi)\times(0,\pi/2)$, and $\Xi>0$ on some open set $U$ of positive measure; since $\rho(\theta,\psi)>0$ almost everywhere and $\sin\theta>0$ for $0<\theta<\pi$, the integrand $\Xi(\theta,\psi)\rho(\theta,\psi)\sin\theta$ is nonnegative almost everywhere and strictly positive on a subset of $U$ of positive measure, so
\[
\int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi/2}\Xi(\theta,\psi)\,\rho(\theta,\psi)\,\sin\theta\,d\psi\,d\theta>0.
\]
Therefore $I$ is the negative of a strictly positive number, hence $I<0$.
