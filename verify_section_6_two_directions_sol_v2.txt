1. **Location:**  
   “Now  
   \[
   A_2-A_1=e_1=-e_3=A_4-A_3,

   and  
   \[
   A_3-A_2=e_2=-e_4=A_4-A_1.

   **Description — Critical Error:**  
   The first displayed equality is false. Since \(e_3=A_4-A_3\), we have
   \[
   -e_3=A_3-A_4,
   \]
   not \(A_4-A_3\). So the chain
   \[
   e_1=-e_3=A_4-A_3
   \]
   is incorrect. As written, this does **not** establish the claimed vector relation for one pair of opposite sides, and the statement “indeed equal as vectors” is not justified.

   \tau_i=\pi-\alpha_{i+1}.
   \]”  
   “Define recursively
   \[
   \varphi_{i+1}:=\varphi_i+\tau_i \qquad (1\le i\le n).

   **Description — Justification Gap:**  
   The proof uses cyclic indexing beyond what was defined. Only \(A_{n+1}=A_1\) was set. But these steps require, at minimum, explicit meaning for the wrap-around cases involving \(A_{n+2}\), \(e_{n+1}\), and \(\alpha_{n+1}\). Without that, the proof does not formally cover the adjacent pair \((e_n,e_1)\), and \(\tau_n\) / \(\varphi_{n+1}\) are not properly defined. This affects Step 1, the alternation argument in Step 2, and the turning-angle argument in Step 3.

The step from “\(n-2\) triangles” to “\(\sum \alpha_i=(n-2)\pi\)” relies on the triangle angle-sum theorem and on the fact that the triangulation’s angles add exactly to the polygon’s interior-angle sum.
