**Bug report**

I found **no Critical Errors**, but under the stated rubric the solution is **not correct** because it still contains the following **Justification Gaps**.

1. **Location:**  
   “Since \(P=\operatorname{conv}(S)\) and \(Q=\operatorname{conv}(T)\),  
   \[
   R=P+Q=\operatorname{conv}(S+T),
   \]
   so \(R\) is a convex polygon.”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The identity
   \[
   \operatorname{conv}(S)+\operatorname{conv}(T)=\operatorname{conv}(S+T)
   \]
   is used without proof. Also, the deduction that this makes \(R\) a genuine 2-dimensional polygon is not justified here.

2. **Location:**  
   “Hence by Lemma 2,
   \[
   F_R(u)=F_P(u)+F_Q(u)=\{s_i+t_i\}=\{x_i\}.
   \]
   Therefore \(x_i\) is a vertex of \(R\).”

   and later

   “Since \(1+\lambda_i>0\) and \(a_i\neq 0\), this is a nondegenerate edge of \(R\).”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The proof silently uses the facts that:
   - a singleton exposed face is a **vertex**, and
   - a nondegenerate exposed face is an **edge**.  
   These identifications are not stated or justified.

3. **Location:**  
   “Because \(P\) is a convex polygon, \(F_P(u)\) is either a vertex \(\{s_i\}\) or an edge \(E_i\).”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   This structural classification of exposed faces of a polygon is invoked without proof. It is essential for the case split in Part (1), Step 3.

4. **Location:**  
   “Therefore the boundary of \(R\) is precisely the closed chain
   \[
   x_1,x_2,\dots,x_n,x_1.
   \]
   Hence the vertices of \(R\) are exactly
   \[
   x_1,x_2,\dots,x_n,
   \]
   they are pairwise distinct, and they occur in that cyclic order.”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   The preceding argument identifies a collection of edge segments, but it does not explicitly justify that these segments run around the boundary in the claimed order, nor that nonadjacent \(x_i\) cannot coincide. Additional argument is needed to conclude pairwise distinctness and cyclic order.

Because the rubric requires **zero** Justification Gaps, the solution does **not** pass verification.
