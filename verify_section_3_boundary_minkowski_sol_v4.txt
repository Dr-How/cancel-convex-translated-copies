**Bug report**

1. **Location:**  
   “The outer-normal rays of the edges \(E_1,\dots,E_n\) of \(P\) are cyclically ordered because the vertices \(s_1,\dots,s_n\) are cyclically ordered. The edge \(e_i\) has the same outer-normal ray as \(E_i\), so the edges
   \[
   e_1,e_2,\dots,e_n
   \]
   occur on the boundary of \(R\) in that same cyclic order.

   Therefore the vertices of \(R\) are
   \[
   e_n\cap e_1,\ e_1\cap e_2,\ \dots,\ e_{n-1}\cap e_n.
   \]”

   **Description:** **Justification Gap.**  
   This is the key step that identifies the boundary order of the edges \(e_i\), but the proof does not justify it. In particular, it does not establish the needed theorem that, for a convex polygon, the cyclic order of edge outer-normal rays determines the cyclic order of the edges along the boundary. Without that justification, the conclusion that \(e_{i-1}\) and \(e_i\) are consecutive boundary edges—and hence that the vertices are exactly \(x_1,\dots,x_n\) in cyclic order—is not fully proved.

No **Critical Errors** found, but because of the above **Justification Gap**, the solution is **not fully verified** under the stated grading rules.
